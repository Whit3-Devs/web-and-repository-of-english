# Diferencias clave entre estructuras en inglés

Este archivo reúne contrastes importantes para ayudarte a:
- no confundir tiempos y estructuras
- elegir mejor qué forma usar
- revisar rápido dudas frecuentes
- hablar y escribir con más precisión

---

## Cómo leer la tabla
- **Structure 1 / Structure 2** = las dos estructuras comparadas
- **Main difference** = diferencia principal
- **Example 1 / Example 2** = ejemplos simples para ver el contraste

---

## 1) Present Simple vs Present Continuous

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Present Simple | Present Continuous | Present Simple = rutinas, hábitos, hechos. Present Continuous = acción ahora o temporal. | I work from home. | I am working from home today. |

### Recordatorio rápido
| Estructura | Uso |
|---|---|
| I work | rutina / hábito |
| I am working | ahora mismo / temporal |

### Señales frecuentes
| Present Simple | Present Continuous |
|---|---|
| always, usually, often, every day | now, right now, at the moment, today |

---

## 2) Past Simple vs Past Continuous

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Past Simple | Past Continuous | Past Simple = acción terminada. Past Continuous = acción en progreso en un momento del pasado. | I studied last night. | I was studying at 8 p.m. |

### Uso combinado típico
| Estructura | Ejemplo |
|---|---|
| Past Continuous + Past Simple | I was studying when you called. |

---

## 3) Past Simple vs Present Perfect

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Past Simple | Present Perfect | Past Simple = pasado terminado con tiempo específico. Present Perfect = experiencia o conexión con el presente. | I saw that movie yesterday. | I have seen that movie before. |

### Regla clave
| Si decís un tiempo terminado... | Usás |
|---|---|
| yesterday, last week, in 2024, two days ago | Past Simple |

| Si no decís cuándo y hablás de experiencia o resultado... | Usás |
|---|---|
| ever, never, already, yet, before | Present Perfect |

---

## 4) Will vs Going to

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| will | be going to | will = decisión del momento, promesa, ofrecimiento, predicción espontánea. going to = plan, intención, algo ya pensado. | I'll answer the phone. | I'm going to study tonight. |

### Recordatorio rápido
| Estructura | Uso |
|---|---|
| I will call him. | decisión del momento |
| I am going to call him. | plan o intención |

---

## 5) Present Simple vs Going to

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Present Simple | be going to | Present Simple = rutina o hecho habitual. Going to = plan futuro. | I study English every day. | I am going to study English tonight. |

---

## 6) Can vs Could

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| can | could | can = habilidad o posibilidad presente. could = habilidad pasada, posibilidad más débil o pedido más amable. | I can swim. | Could you help me? |

### Recordatorio rápido
| Estructura | Uso |
|---|---|
| can | presente / habilidad / permiso informal |
| could | pasado / pedido más educado / posibilidad |

---

## 7) Can vs Should

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| can | should | can = capacidad o posibilidad. should = consejo o recomendación. | I can finish it today. | You should rest. |

---

## 8) Must vs Have to

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| must | have to | ambos expresan obligación, pero must suele sonar más personal o fuerte; have to, más general o externa. | I must call my mom. | I have to go to work tomorrow. |

### Nota útil
| Estructura | Idea común |
|---|---|
| must | obligación fuerte / decisión del hablante |
| have to | necesidad externa / regla / obligación práctica |

---

## 9) For vs Since

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| for | since | for = duración. since = punto de inicio. | I have lived here for two years. | I have lived here since 2024. |

### Recordatorio rápido
| Estructura | Uso |
|---|---|
| for two hours | duración |
| since Monday | inicio |

---

## 10) In vs On vs At (tiempo)

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 / 3 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| in | on / at | in = meses, años, partes del día. on = días y fechas. at = hora exacta. | I was born in 1998. | We met on Monday at 8 p.m. |

### Resumen rápido
| Preposition | Uso |
|---|---|
| in | in July, in 2026, in the morning |
| on | on Monday, on my birthday, on May 3 |
| at | at 7 p.m., at night, at noon |

---

## 11) Say vs Tell

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| say | tell | say = decir algo. tell = decirle algo a alguien. | She said hello. | She told me the truth. |

### Recordatorio rápido
| Estructura | Patrón |
|---|---|
| say + something | say goodbye |
| tell + someone + something | tell me a story |

---

## 12) Do vs Make

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| do | make | do = actividades, tareas, trabajo. make = crear, producir, preparar. | I do my homework. | I make dinner. |

### Ejemplos frecuentes
| do | make |
|---|---|
| do homework | make coffee |
| do exercise | make a mistake |
| do the dishes | make a plan |

---

## 13) Much vs Many

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| much | many | much = incontables. many = contables. | I don't have much time. | I don't have many friends here. |

---

## 14) Some vs Any

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| some | any | some = afirmativas y ofrecimientos. any = negativas y preguntas en general. | I have some questions. | Do you have any questions? |

---

## 15) So vs Because

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| so | because | because = razón. so = consecuencia. | I stayed home because I was sick. | I was sick, so I stayed home. |

---

## 16) Although vs But

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| although | but | ambos contrastan ideas, pero no suelen usarse juntos en la misma estructura simple. | Although I was tired, I went out. | I was tired, but I went out. |

---

## 17) Also vs Too vs As well

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 / 3 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| also | too / as well | also suele ir en el medio. too y as well suelen ir al final. | She also speaks English. | She speaks English too. |

---

## 18) This / That / These / Those

| Structure 1 | Structure 2 | Main difference | Example 1 | Example 2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| this / these | that / those | this/these = cerca. that/those = lejos. this/that singular, these/those plural. | This book is mine. | Those shoes are nice. |

---

## Errores frecuentes en estas diferencias

| Common confusion | Correct use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I have seen it yesterday. | Past Simple con tiempo terminado. | I saw it yesterday. |
| I will to call you. | Después de will va verbo base. | I will call you. |
| I am here since Monday. | Since suele ir con present perfect. | I have been here since Monday. |
| She said me the truth. | Con persona, normalmente usás tell. | She told me the truth. |
| I have many time. | Time es incontable. | I don't have much time. |
