# Future Perfect Continuous

## Main Use
Use it for the duration of an activity up to a future point.

## Timeline Idea / Visual Meaning
The focus is on how long something will have been happening before a future moment.

```text
PAST ---- NOW ====>>>> [future point]
          duration up to future point
```

## Structure
- Affirmative: **Subject + will have been + verb-ing**
- Negative: **Subject + will not have been + verb-ing**
- Question: **Will + subject + have been + verb-ing?**

## Examples
- By next June, I will have been working here for five years.
- She won't have been studying long by the exam date.
- Will they have been living there for a decade by then?

## Common Markers / Signals
- for
- by
- by the time
- by next year
- for five years

## Contrast
Compare with [[Future Perfect]] when you want duration, not just completion.

## Common Mistakes
- **I will have been work here for years.** ❌
- **I will have been working here for years.** ✅

- **She will have been lived here for years.** ❌
- **She will have been living here for years.** ✅
