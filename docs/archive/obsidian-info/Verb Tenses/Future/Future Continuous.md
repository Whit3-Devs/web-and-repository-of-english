# Future Continuous

## Main Use
Use it for an action that will be in progress at a specific moment in the future.

## Timeline Idea / Visual Meaning
The action will be happening around a future point.

```text
PAST ---- NOW ---- [future point: in progress] ---->
```

## Structure
- Affirmative: **Subject + will be + verb-ing**
- Negative: **Subject + will not be + verb-ing**
- Question: **Will + subject + be + verb-ing?**

## Examples
- This time tomorrow, I will be flying to Córdoba.
- She won't be working at 9 p.m.
- Will they be waiting for us when we arrive?

## Common Markers / Signals
- this time tomorrow
- at 8 p.m. tomorrow
- next week at this time
- when you arrive

## Contrast
Compare with [[Future Simple]] when you want an action in progress instead of a simple future event.

## Common Mistakes
- **I will be study at 9.** ❌
- **I will be studying at 9.** ✅

- **Will be you working later?** ❌
- **Will you be working later?** ✅
