# Present Simple

## Main Use
Use it for routines, habits, facts, and things that are generally true.

## Timeline Idea / Visual Meaning
Think of it as a stable line around "always / usually / every day".
It does not focus on one exact moment. It shows repetition, routine, or general truth.

```text
PAST -------- NOW -------- FUTURE
            [routine / fact]
```

## Structure
- Affirmative: **Subject + base verb / base verb + s**
- Negative: **Subject + do/does not + base verb**
- Question: **Do/Does + subject + base verb?**

## Examples
- She works from home.
- They do not study at night.
- Does he play tennis on Sundays?

## Common Markers / Signals
- always
- usually
- often
- sometimes
- every day
- on Mondays

## Contrast
Compare with [[Present Continuous]] when you want to separate routine from action happening now.

## Common Mistakes
- **She don't work here.** ❌
- **She doesn't work here.** ✅

- **Does he works here?** ❌
- **Does he work here?** ✅
