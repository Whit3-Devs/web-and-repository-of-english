# Present Perfect

## Main Use
Use it for actions or experiences that connect the past with the present.

## Timeline Idea / Visual Meaning
The action is linked to **now**. The exact finished time is not the focus.

```text
PAST --------> NOW
      connection / result now
```

## Structure
- Affirmative: **Subject + have/has + past participle**
- Negative: **Subject + have/has not + past participle**
- Question: **Have/Has + subject + past participle?**

## Examples
- I have finished my homework.
- She hasn't seen that movie.
- Have they arrived yet?

## Common Markers / Signals
- already
- yet
- ever
- never
- just
- before

## Contrast
Compare with [[Past Simple]] when you want to separate a finished past time from a past action that still matters now.

## Common Mistakes
- **I have seen it yesterday.** ❌
- **I saw it yesterday.** ✅

- **She has went home.** ❌
- **She has gone home.** ✅
