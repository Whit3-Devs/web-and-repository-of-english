# Advice and Obligation

Use this note when you want to give advice, make a recommendation, or express a strong obligation.

Back to: [[Modal Verbs Overview]]

---

## Core idea

For advice and obligation, the most useful modals are:

- **should**
- **ought to**
- **must**

The base structure is usually:

**Subject + modal + base verb + complement**

With **ought to**, the pattern includes **to**:

**Subject + ought to + base verb + complement**

---

## Should

Use **should** for advice or recommendation.

Examples:
- **You should rest.**
- **You should practice every day.**
- **We should review the notes before speaking.**

Negative:
- **You should not worry too much.**
- **You shouldn't skip practice.**

---

## Ought to

Use **ought to** for advice, moral duty, or what feels right. It is similar to **should**, but a bit more formal.

Examples:
- **You ought to apologize.**
- **We ought to be honest.**
- **He ought to study the basics first.**

---

## Must

Use **must** for strong obligation or necessity.

Examples:
- **You must wear a seatbelt.**
- **We must finish this today.**
- **You must understand the fundamentals first.**

Negative:
- **You must not smoke here.**
- **You mustn't share your password.**

> [!warning]
> **Must not** means prohibition, not lack of obligation.
>
> **You must not do this** = no tenés permitido hacerlo.

---

## Quick contrast

| Intention | Modal | Example |
|---|---|---|
| advice | **should** | **You should practice.** |
| formal/moral advice | **ought to** | **You ought to tell the truth.** |
| strong obligation | **must** | **You must be careful.** |
| prohibition | **must not** | **You must not touch this.** |

---

## Common mistakes

| Mistake | Better |
|---|---|
| **You should to study.** | **You should study.** |
| **He musts go.** | **He must go.** |
| **You ought study.** | **You ought to study.** |
