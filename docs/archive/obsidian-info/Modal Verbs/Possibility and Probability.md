# Possibility and Probability

Use this note when you want to say that something is possible, probable, uncertain, or logically likely.

Back to: [[Modal Verbs Overview]]

---

## Core idea

For possibility and probability, the most useful modals are:

- **may**
- **might**
- **could**
- **must** for logical deduction

The base structure stays the same:

**Subject + modal + base verb + complement**

---

## May

Use **may** when something is possible.

Examples:
- **It may rain later.**
- **She may join the meeting.**
- **This approach may work.**

---

## Might

Use **might** when the possibility feels weaker or more uncertain.

Examples:
- **It might rain later.**
- **They might not understand the requirement.**
- **I might practice tonight.**

---

## Could

Use **could** when something is possible as one option among others.

Examples:
- **This could be the problem.**
- **We could try another solution.**
- **The issue could come from the cache.**

---

## Must as deduction

Use **must** when you are making a strong logical conclusion.

Examples:
- **You must be tired.**
- **The server must be down.**
- **She must know the answer.**

> [!warning]
> **Must** here is not obligation. It means: “based on the evidence, this is probably true.”

---

## Quick contrast

| Intention | Modal | Example |
|---|---|---|
| possible | **may** | **It may work.** |
| weaker possibility | **might** | **It might work.** |
| possible option | **could** | **This could be the reason.** |
| strong deduction | **must** | **This must be the reason.** |

---

## Common mistakes

| Mistake | Better |
|---|---|
| **It might rains.** | **It might rain.** |
| **This could to work.** | **This could work.** |
| **You must are tired.** | **You must be tired.** |
