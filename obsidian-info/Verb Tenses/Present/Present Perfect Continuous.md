# Present Perfect Continuous

## Main Use
Use it for actions that started in the past and continue until now, or that recently stopped but still show a result now.

## Timeline Idea / Visual Meaning
The focus is on **duration up to now**.

```text
PAST ========> NOW
    duration / ongoing activity
```

## Structure
- Affirmative: **Subject + have/has been + verb-ing**
- Negative: **Subject + have/has not been + verb-ing**
- Question: **Have/Has + subject + been + verb-ing?**

## Examples
- I have been studying for two hours.
- He hasn't been sleeping well lately.
- Have you been working on that project?

## Common Markers / Signals
- for
- since
- lately
- all day
- recently

## Contrast
Compare with [[Present Perfect]] when you want to focus on duration instead of result.

## Common Mistakes
- **I am living here since 2022.** ❌
- **I have been living here since 2022.** ✅

- **She has been work all day.** ❌
- **She has been working all day.** ✅
