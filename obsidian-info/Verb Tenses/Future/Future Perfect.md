# Future Perfect

## Main Use
Use it for an action that will be completed before a future point.

## Timeline Idea / Visual Meaning
The action finishes before another future moment.

```text
PAST ---- NOW ---- [finished before future point] --->
```

## Structure
- Affirmative: **Subject + will have + past participle**
- Negative: **Subject + will not have + past participle**
- Question: **Will + subject + have + past participle?**

## Examples
- They will have finished the project by Friday.
- She won't have arrived by 6 p.m.
- Will you have completed the course by July?

## Common Markers / Signals
- by
- by the time
- before
- by Friday
- by next month

## Contrast
Compare with [[Future Simple]] when you need to show completion before a future deadline.

## Common Mistakes
- **I will have finish it by tomorrow.** ❌
- **I will have finished it by tomorrow.** ✅

- **She will has completed it.** ❌
- **She will have completed it.** ✅
